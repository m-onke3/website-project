Product Owner

A product owner in Agile is responsible for maximizing the value delivered by the team by ensuring that the team backlog is aligned with customer and stakeholder needs. They act as the team's primary customer advocate and link to business and technology strategy. They participate in daily scrums, sprint planning, sprint reviews, and retrospectives, and strive to understand the needs of stakeholders and the vision for the finished product.

Key Responsibilities

  • Defining and prioritising the product backlog
  • Communicating the product vision
  • Collaborating with stakeholders
  • Participating in sprint planning
  • Providing guidance and clarification
  • Accepting and validating deliverables
  • Adapting and refining the product backlog

Scrum Master

A Scrum Master is a facilitator and coach for an Agile development team, responsible for establishing and maintaining the Scrum framework, coaching team members in self-management and cross-functionality, and ensuring that all Scrum events take place and are positive, productive, and kept within the timebox.

Key Responsibilities

  • Facilitating the scrum process
  • Coaching the team
  • Removing impediments
  • Facilitating communcation
  • Supporting the product owner
  • Promoting Agile values and principles
  • Facilitating team meetings

Development Team

The development team is responsible for creating the product, which includes all the work required to deliver a potentially releasable product at the end of each sprint. The development team is self-organizing and cross-functional, and includes professionals who do the hands-on work of completing the tasks in a Scrum sprint, e.g engineers, testers, designers, QA team

Key Responsibilities

  • Sprint execution
  • Self organisation
  • Cross functionality
  • Collaboration
  • Accountability
  • Continous Improvement

Stakeholders

The stakeholders are those who have an interest in the project that is being undertaken. They can be internal or external to the organization, and they may have a financial, operational, or strategic interest in the project.
